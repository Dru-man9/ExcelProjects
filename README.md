📡 TMT Financial Analysis
A Financial Analysis of 11 Companies in the Telecommunications, Media & Technology Industry

FIN 315003 — Orange Team | Charles, Dhruva, Sid, Matthew, Suvil | December 2025


📋 Overview
This project presents a comprehensive financial analysis of 11 major TMT companies spanning a 10-year period (2015–2024). The analysis covers revenue trends, EBITDA growth, total assets, employee headcount, enterprise value, and market capitalization across three industry segments: Technology, Media, and Telecommunications.
Key headline figures:

📈 Revenue Growth (2015–2024): +130%
💰 EBITDA Growth (2015–2024): +177%
💵 Total industry revenue grew from $1T → $2.3T

📊 Key Findings
Industry-Wide Trends

Total revenue across all companies more than doubled from 2015 to 2024
Total assets also more than doubled, led by Apple, Amazon, and Google
Combined employment peaked at ~2.75M in 2021, declining slightly to ~2.6M by 2024

Standout Performers

Amazon: Most dramatic growth story — revenue $107B → $638B, EBITDA $8.5B → $121B, workforce 231K → 1.56M
Meta: Revenue $17.9B → $164.5B; significant dip in 2022 followed by strong recovery
Google: Revenue $75B → $350B; EV grew from $1.19T → $2.33T
Netflix: Revenue $6.8B → $39B with steady subscriber-driven growth
T-Mobile: Strongest telecom growth driven by the 2020 Sprint merger

Telecom Sector

Verizon & AT&T show stable revenues but declining market caps and significant workforce reductions
T-Mobile stands apart with accelerating EBITDA and rising valuation post-merger

Media Sector

Warner Bros. Discovery revenues surged post-2022 merger but declining market cap signals investor uncertainty around streaming economics and debt load
Sony reached its highest market cap and assets in 2023

📌 Methodology
All financial figures are sourced from publicly available annual reports and financial databases. Key metrics analyzed include:

Revenue / Sales (annual, in USD millions/billions)
EBITDA (earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation & amortization)
Total Assets
Employee Headcount
Enterprise Value (EV)
Market Capitalization

Data spans fiscal years 2015–2024, with individual company deep-dives focused on 2020–2024 for granularity.
